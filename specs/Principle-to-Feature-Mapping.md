## SECTION 1: Principle-to-Feature Mapping

### 1.1 Values & Purpose Discovery

**Source principles:** Brain dump what matters → narrow to top 10 core values → write a concise purpose statement → translate values into identity and actionable priorities. 75-value brainstorming list as selection tool. Identity-based habits: start with who you want to become, not what you want to achieve. Begin with the end in mind (Covey). Mental box programming (Long). Consciousness calibration — discerning what resonates at the deepest level (Hawkins).

**Where it lives in MyAIM:**

| Feature | PRD | Role | Status |
|---------|-----|------|--------|
| Guiding Stars — "Let LiLa Help Me Discover Mine" | PRD-06 | Values discovery interview, declaration crafting with honest commitment language | Designed |
| Guiding Stars — "Craft with LiLa" | PRD-06 | Ongoing declaration creation for existing users | Designed |
| LifeLantern — Assessment + Vision + Personal Vision Statement | PRD-12A | Deeper periodic layer: 13 life areas, gap analysis, cohesive vision narrative | Designed |
| Values Brainstorming List | NEW | Browsable AI Vault tool + LiLa coaching resource during Guiding Stars discovery | New |
| Declaration Starter Patterns | NEW | Browsable collection of honest commitment sentence starters organized by theme | New |

**What's already in PRDs:** The discovery interview, declaration language philosophy (honest commitments vs. hollow affirmations), all 4 Guiding Stars entry types, LifeLantern's 13 life areas with assessment/vision/gap analysis/goal generation, Personal Vision Statement synthesis.

**What's new:**
- **Values Brainstorming List** — 75+ values/priorities organized by category (Relationships & Connection, Personal Well-Being, Spiritual & Inner Life, Growth & Development, Achievement & Impact, Lifestyle & Freedom, Character & Ethics). Browsable in AI Vault AND used by LiLa during the Guiding Stars discovery flow. LiLa can offer it: "Would it help to look at a list of values to see what jumps out at you?"
- **Declaration Starter Patterns** — Browsable collection of honest commitment sentence openers organized by identity area (parenting, faith, health, character, relationships, finances, purpose). Not finished declarations — starting-point phrases like "I am choosing to...", "I carry...", "I hold fast to..." Users can browse independently or LiLa can weave them into the crafting conversation.

**Principle alignment:**
- Identity-based habits (Clear) → Declaration language IS the identity reframe. "I am choosing to become someone who responds with patience" is functionally identical to "I am the type of person who stays calm." MyAIM's honesty test makes it stronger.
- Begin with the end in mind (Covey) → LifeLantern vision casting. The funeral exercise from Long's FEAR process maps directly to LifeLantern's vision questions.
- Mental box programming (Long) → Guiding Stars declarations ARE the positive mental programming. The morning rhythm rotation of Guiding Stars serves the same function as Long's "morning quiet time reading affirmations" — except MyAIM uses honest commitments, not hollow affirmations.
- Consciousness calibration (Hawkins) → Discernment as a core MyAIM value. The values discovery process helps users calibrate what truly resonates at the deepest level, not just what sounds good.

### 1.2 Morning Planning Ritual

**Source principles:** Short focused block at the start of the day to set direction. Review incoming tasks/ideas. Identify top high-value priorities. Align the day's actions with values. Creates clarity and prevents reactive chaos. Various "prioritize first" frameworks (top 5, eat the frog, big rocks). Proactive living — choose response to circumstances rather than reacting (Covey). Mental box review (Long).

**Where it lives in MyAIM:**

| Feature | PRD | Role | Status |
|---------|-----|------|--------|
| Morning Rhythm (default template) | PRD-18 (in progress) | Daily dashboard card with breathing glow, configurable sections | In progress |
| Guiding Stars rotation in greeting header | PRD-14 | Rotating declaration in dashboard greeting | Designed |
| Best Intentions in morning rhythm | PRD-06 Screen 4 | 2-3 focus intentions for today | Designed (data contract) |
| 13 prioritization views on dashboard | PRD-09A | Task views auto-sorted by user preference | Designed |
| Smart Notepad quick capture | PRD-08 | "Anything on your mind?" capture field | Designed |

**Default Morning Rhythm template sections (recommended for PRD-18):**

1. **Anchoring Moment** — One rotating Guiding Star declaration. Framing: "Remember who you are..." (PRD-06 already defines this)
2. **Focus Intentions** — 2-3 active Best Intentions selected for today. Random rotation, priority-weighted, or user-pinned. Framing: "Today, remember to..." (PRD-06 already defines this)
3. **Today's Priorities** — Surfaces the user's top tasks using their preferred prioritization view. If they use Eat the Frog, show the frog. If 1-3-5, show the structure. If Simple List, show top items. Does NOT prescribe a specific number or framework — surfaces whatever the user has chosen.
4. **Today's Calendar** — Day's events at a glance (stub until Calendar PRD)
5. **Quick Capture** — "Anything on your mind?" text field that routes to Smart Notepad. The single-capture-location principle.
6. **Periodic Cards (conditional)** — Friday Overview, Monthly Review, or Quarterly Inventory cards appear here on their respective days

**Architecture:** Sections are categories. Mom can enable/disable sections, reorder them, and design custom section content per family member. A kid's morning rhythm might show: encouraging message + today's routine checklist + fun fact. A teen's might show: today's tasks + calendar + quick capture. Mom's might show all 6 sections. The default template is just the starting point.

**What's new:** The section-based template composition for PRD-18. The data contracts are already in PRDs — this mapping provides the content rationale for why these specific sections exist in the default.

### 1.3 Evening Reflection Ritual

**Source principles:** Short focused block at end of day to review accomplishments, note wins and gratitude, capture unfinished tasks/ideas, plan tomorrow's priorities. Closes the day positively, reduces mental clutter, builds momentum. "Make it satisfying" (Clear). Reflection as the mechanism for growth — pain + reflection = progress (Dalio). Evening gratitude as identity reinforcement.

**Where it lives in MyAIM:**

| Feature | PRD | Role | Status |
|---------|-----|------|--------|
| Evening Rhythm (default template) | PRD-18 (in progress) | Daily dashboard card with breathing glow, configurable sections | In progress |
| Victory Recorder summary in evening | PRD-11 | Today's victories, celebration trigger | Designed (data contract) |
| Best Intentions check-in in evening | PRD-06 Screen 4 | Iteration counts, gentle handling of 0-tap intentions | Designed (data contract) |
| Reflections (Journal sub-page) | PRD-08 | Container slot defined, content deferred | Container designed, content new |
| Smart Notepad brain dump | PRD-08 | "Anything still on your mind?" capture | Designed |

**Default Evening Rhythm template sections (recommended for PRD-18):**

1. **Victory Summary** — Today's Victory Recorder entries. Tap "Celebrate This!" for full LiLa narrative. NEVER shows what wasn't done. (PRD-11 data contract)
2. **Intention Check-in** — Each active Best Intention with today's tap count. 0-tap intentions shown gently: "Didn't get to this one today — that's okay. Tomorrow's a new day." (PRD-06 data contract)
3. **Reflections** — Rotating prompts from a customizable library. User answers at least 3 (guideline, not enforcement). Each answer routes to Journal as a Reflection entry type with optional secondary routing to Victory Recorder, Gratitude sub-tag, or LifeLantern area. See Section 3 for the complete reflection prompt library.
4. **Brain Dump** — "Anything still on your mind?" field routing to Smart Notepad. The mental clutter release.
5. **Tomorrow Prep (optional)** — Preview of tomorrow's calendar + suggested priority selection. Plants the seed so morning rhythm can confirm rather than decide from scratch.

**Architecture:** Same section-based customizable system as morning. Mom designs per-member. A kid's evening might show: victories + one reflection question + encouraging close. Guided mode kids get the DailyCelebration sequence (PRD-11) instead of the adult evening rhythm.

**Design decision: Incomplete task triage does NOT belong in the evening rhythm.** Victory Recorder's core philosophy is "never show what wasn't done." Incomplete tasks belong in the morning rhythm's priority check or a separate task review — not the celebration/reflection space.

### 1.4 Weekly Review + Renewal

**Source principles:** Step back from daily grind to review weekly progress, assess alignment with values and goals, adjust course. Both practical review (what got done, what carried, what's coming) and renewal/reflection (gratitude, perspective, recommitment). The weekly sharpen-the-saw renewal (Covey). Systematic review and reflection cycle (Dalio).

**Where it lives in MyAIM:**

| Feature | PRD | Role | Status |
|---------|-----|------|--------|
| Friday Overview (periodic rhythm) | PRD-18 (in progress) | Quick stats card in morning rhythm on configured day | Architecture from StewardShip |
| Sunday Reflection (periodic rhythm) | PRD-18 (in progress) | Renewal/reflection overlay on configured day | Architecture from StewardShip |
| Weekly Review meeting type | PRD-16 | Deep-dive structured conversation with agenda/action items | Designed |

**Two depths, one principle:**
- **Friday Overview** (rhythm card) — Glanceable. Week stats: tasks completed/carried/cancelled, top victories, active streaks, next week preview. 60-second awareness moment. Appears as an inline card within the morning rhythm.
- **Sunday Reflection** (rhythm card or overlay) — Renewal. Rotates through Guiding Stars, surfaces a LifeLantern area that hasn't been visited in a while, offers a reflection prompt, includes intention-setting for the week ahead. Configurable day (not everyone does Sunday). Configurable content (not everyone does spiritual reflection).
- **Weekly Review meeting** (PRD-16 deep dive) — The sit-down conversation. LiLa guides through agenda sections: this week's wins (Victory Recorder data), tasks carried forward, alignment check against Guiding Stars, and priority-setting for next week. The Friday Overview card can include "Want to go deeper?" link to start this meeting.

**Open design question:** Both the Friday and Sunday rhythms should be moveable to any day of the week, with fully editable content. Someone might prefer "Monday Planning" and "Saturday Renewal." The default names and days are just starter positions.

### 1.5 90-Day Quarterly Planning Cycles

**Source principles:** 90-day planning sprints rather than overwhelming annual goals. Review values/purpose → set big-picture vision → choose 3-7 goals → identify high-impact actions → break into milestones → track progress → adjust monthly. Begin with the end in mind (Covey). Focus on what you want, be specific (Long). Systematic goal-setting with measurable outcomes.

**Where it lives in MyAIM:**

| Feature | PRD | Role | Status |
|---------|-----|------|--------|
| LifeLantern — assessment/vision/gap/goals | PRD-12A | Per-area review, vision casting, goal generation | Designed |
| Quarterly Inventory (periodic rhythm) | PRD-18 (in progress) | Nudge card when ~90 days since last LifeLantern | Architecture from StewardShip |
| Project Planner (Rigging) | Future PRD | Multi-step plans with milestones | Stub in PRD-12A |
| Monthly Review (periodic rhythm) | PRD-18 (in progress) | Monthly stats card linking to reports | Architecture from StewardShip |

**How the cycle works in MyAIM:**
1. Quarterly Inventory rhythm card appears in morning rhythm → nudges user to LifeLantern
2. LifeLantern assessment updates current reality per life area
3. LifeLantern vision casting updates aspirational future
4. Gap analysis identifies what's changed
5. Goal generation suggests concrete actions → routes to Guiding Stars (identity), Best Intentions (practices), Tasks (actions), Widgets/Trackers (measurables), or Project Planner (complex plans)
6. Monthly Review rhythm card shows progress at-a-glance each month
7. Repeat next quarter

**LiLa coaching beat:** "Let's focus on what feels achievable in the next quarter. What would make the biggest difference in this area over the next three months?" This avoids prescribing a specific number of goals and honors the buffet principle — user engages with whatever areas and depth feel right.

### 1.6 Breaking Big Goals into Micro-Actions

**Source principles:** Every big goal breaks into micro-actions (≤20 minutes). 2-Minute Rule: scale any habit to ≤2 minutes to start (Clear). Reverse engineering — start from end state, work backwards. Straight-line action — shortest path between current position and desired outcome (Djukich). 80/20 rule — identify the 20% of actions that produce 80% of results (Long).

**Where it lives in MyAIM:**

| Feature | PRD | Role | Status |
|---------|-----|------|--------|
| Task Breaker | PRD-09A | AI decomposition into subtasks at 3 detail levels | Designed |
| LifeLantern goal generation | PRD-12A | Gap analysis → concrete action suggestions | Designed |
| Project Planner (Rigging) | Future PRD | Multi-step plans with milestones, backward planning | Stub |

**LiLa coaching behaviors (NEW — Pattern Library entries):**
- **2-Minute Rule:** "This is a great goal. What's the smallest possible version of getting started — something you could do in two minutes?" Used during Best Intentions creation, task creation, and goal generation.
- **Straight-Line Check:** "What's the single next action that would move this forward the most?" Used when someone is overwhelmed or spinning on a complex goal.
- **80/20 Lens:** "Of everything on your plate, which 2-3 items would make the biggest difference if they were the only things you accomplished?" Used during morning rhythm priority-setting and weekly review.
- **Backward Planning:** "When does this need to be done? Let's work backwards from there." Used in Project Planner and complex task decomposition.

### 1.9 Atomic Habits Four Laws as LiLa Coaching Behavior

**Source principles:** Make it Obvious (Cue) → Make it Attractive (Craving) → Make it Easy (Response) → Make it Satisfying (Reward). Inversions for breaking bad habits. Habit stacking. Environment design.

**This is NOT a single feature — it's a coaching philosophy across the entire platform.**

| Law | MyAIM Feature Mapping |
|-----|----------------------|
| **Make it Obvious** | Rhythms (breathing glow cue), Dashboard (GS rotation, BI widget, task section), Best Intentions rotating through widgets |
| **Make it Attractive** | Declaration language (identity framing), LiLa context presets (personally compelling suggestions), temptation bundling as coaching technique |
| **Make it Easy** | Task Breaker (decompose to tiny steps), 2-Minute Rule coaching, Smart Notepad (single capture), Routines (reduce decision fatigue), Quick-action buttons everywhere |
| **Make it Satisfying** | Victory Recorder celebrations, BI confetti tap, Widget streaks/star charts, Gamification (PRD-24) |
| **Habit Stacking** | Routines with sections (morning routine IS a habit stack), Rhythms as anchors, LiLa coaching: "After X, do Y" |
| **Environment Design** | Dashboard layout customization, Widget placement, Rhythm timing configuration |
| **Inversions** | LiLa coaching: make cue invisible, behavior unattractive, add barriers, make it unsatisfying |

**What's new:** The explicit framing of the Four Laws as a **LiLa coaching pattern set** for the Pattern Library. Each law becomes a coaching tool LiLa draws on situationally — not a rigid framework, but a toolkit. See Document 2 for detailed conversation flows.

### 1.10 Identity-Based Habits → Declaration Language

**Source principles:** Strongest behavioral change starts with identity ("I am the type of person who..."), not outcomes. Every action is a vote for the person you're becoming. Strategy of Identity (Rubin). Mental box programming (Long).

**Where it lives in MyAIM:** Already deeply embedded in Guiding Stars (PRD-06) via the declaration language philosophy.

**The architectural loop that embodies this:**
1. **Declare** who you're becoming → Guiding Stars
2. **Practice** the associated behaviors → Best Intentions
3. **Generate evidence** through action → Victory Recorder (auto-captured + manual)
4. **Connect evidence back to declarations** → Victory Recorder Guiding Stars thread detection
5. **Reinforce identity** through celebration narrative → "That's your 'I pursue wisdom like a hidden treasure' star shining through."

This is the complete identity → action → evidence → reinforcement loop, already designed across PRDs 06, 11, and the LiLa context assembly pipeline.

### 1.12 Compound Effect / 1% Better Daily

**Source principles:** Small consistent actions compound into massive results. 1% improvement philosophy. Time as most valuable currency. Consistent short rituals > occasional intensive efforts. Marginal gains (Clear). Consistency over intensity (Long).

**This is the platform's core thesis, not a single feature:**
- Rhythms = the short daily rituals
- Best Intentions = daily micro-practices
- Victory Recorder = makes compound effect visible by accumulating evidence
- Widgets/Trackers = visualize streaks and trends
- LifeLantern snapshot history = growth across quarters
- LiLa celebration narratives = "This is the 14th day in a row. That's not luck — that's who you are now."

**LiLa coaching pattern (NEW):** When someone is discouraged: "You only did 5 minutes today" → "Five minutes is 5 minutes more than zero. Consistency matters more than intensity. You showed up." Acknowledge the difficulty FIRST, then offer evidence from Victory Recorder.

### 1.16 Parenting Frameworks & Child Communication

**Source principles:** Children are good inside — behavior stems from underlying struggles, not badness (Kennedy). Most generous interpretation — "What is the most generous interpretation of what just happened?" (Kennedy). Two things are true simultaneously — firm AND warm, boundaries AND love (Kennedy). Acknowledge feelings before addressing behavior (Faber). Give feelings a name (Faber). Wishes in fantasy — "I wish I could make that happen for you right now" (Faber). Accept all feelings, limit harmful actions (Faber). Intentional family activities with anticipation (Peck). Identity-driven behavior — clear roles provide security and identity (Peck). Self-government — teaching children to govern their own behavior rather than external control (Peck).

**Where it lives in MyAIM:**

**LiLa coaching principles from these frameworks:**
- **Good Inside principle:** When mom reports difficult child behavior, LiLa's first response should be grounded in "What is your most generous interpretation of what just happened?" Not dismissive of the behavior — two things can be true: the behavior is unacceptable AND the child is struggling with something underneath.
- **Acknowledge before solving:** LiLa coaches mom to name the child's feeling BEFORE addressing the behavior. "It sounds like he's feeling really frustrated" before "Here's how to handle it."
- **Wishes in fantasy:** When a child can't have something, coaching mom to say "I wish I could make that happen for you" before explaining why not. This is a powerful validation tool.
- **Identity-driven behavior (Peck):** LiLa can help mom establish family roles and expectations that give children a sense of identity and purpose rather than just rule-following. "When kids know WHO they are in the family, they know HOW to act."
- **Self-government (Peck):** Routines, tasks, and opportunities in PRD-09A already support this — children learn to manage their own behavior within a structured system rather than requiring constant external prompting.

**What's new:** These parenting frameworks as explicit LiLa coaching patterns for the Pattern Library. They inform how LiLa coaches in Higgins Navigate (parent-child), during meeting facilitation, and in general chat when mom discusses parenting challenges.

### 1.17 Sibling Relationships & Multi-Child Dynamics

**Source principles:** Validate ALL sibling feelings including negative ones — anger, jealousy, and hatred are natural responses to feeling displaced (Faber). Channel hostility into expression, not suppression (Faber). Avoid comparison — describe what you see rather than evaluating ("I see you worked hard on that" not "Yours is better than your sister's"). Unique, not equal — each child gets what they need, not identical treatment (Faber). Stop hurtful behavior immediately while honoring the feeling (Faber).

**Where it lives in MyAIM:**

| Feature | PRD | Role | Status |
|---------|-----|------|--------|
| Best Intentions (sibling-tagged) | PRD-06 | Intentions related to specific sibling relationships | Designed |
| Higgins Navigate | PRD-05 guided mode | Coaching for navigating sibling conflicts | Designed |
| Family Meeting | PRD-16 | Family conversations that include sibling dynamics | Designed |
| Tasks — per-member assignment | PRD-09A | Fair distribution, not identical | Designed |

**LiLa coaching principles:**
- When mom describes sibling conflict, LiLa coaches toward validating both children's feelings rather than immediately mediating: "Both kids are hurting right now. What if you started by naming what each one is feeling?"
- **Unique not equal:** LiLa can help mom see that fairness means each child getting what THEY need, which will look different. This connects to the Play/Guided/Independent modes — different children get different experiences because they need different things.
- Best Intentions tagged to specific sibling relationships (like "foster Gideon & Mosiah's relationship") give LiLa context to suggest collaborative approaches to daily tasks and activities.

### 1.18 Self-Deception, Self-Betrayal & Personal Ownership

**Source principles:** Self-betrayal occurs when we violate our inner sense of right and wrong, particularly in how we treat others (Warner). Self-deception blocks growth — we create our emotional problems while deceiving ourselves about our role (Warner). Heart-based morality requires wholeheartedness, not just correct behavior — we must BE honorable, not just ACT honorably (Warner). Inward vs. outward mindset — seeing others as people with their own needs/challenges vs. objects in our way (Arbinger). Mindset before behavior — sustainable change requires mindset shift, not just behavior modification (Arbinger). The U-turn — shift focus from changing others to examining yourself (Schwartz/IFS). Radical responsibility — you are 100% responsible for what's in your mental box (Long).

**Where it lives in MyAIM:**

| Feature | PRD | Role | Status |
|---------|-----|------|--------|
| ThoughtSift | PRD-05 guided mode | Deep personal processing and honest self-examination | Designed |
| InnerWorkings | PRD-07 | Self-knowledge: personality, strengths, how I process | Designed |
| Guiding Stars — declarations | PRD-06 | Honest commitments that require authentic self-examination | Designed |
| LifeLantern — assessment | PRD-12A | Honest current-reality assessment per life area | Designed |
| Couple Meeting | PRD-16 | Structured relationship conversations | Designed |
| Journal / Reflections | PRD-08 + PRD-18 | Evening reflection with accountability questions | Designed (container) + New (prompts) |

**This is a foundational coaching philosophy, not a single feature.** It runs through everything:

- **ThoughtSift** is the primary home for this work. When someone is stuck in blame or frustration, ThoughtSift can gently guide toward self-examination: "I hear how frustrated you are with [person]. Before we go further — what's your part in this situation? Not to blame yourself, but to find what's actually within your power to change."
- **LifeLantern assessment** requires honest self-examination by design. LiLa's assessment questions are calibrated to draw out genuine current reality, not the version that sounds good.
- **Guiding Stars honesty test** — declarations must be true RIGHT NOW. This prevents the self-deception of claiming a finished state you haven't reached.
- **Evening reflection prompts** — "Where did I fall short today, and what would I do differently?" and "What did I avoid today that I know I need to face?" are direct self-betrayal awareness questions.

**LiLa coaching principles (NEW):**
- **Outward Mindset Check:** When someone describes interpersonal conflict entirely as the other person's fault, LiLa can gently introduce the mindset question: "How might they be experiencing this situation? What might they need that you haven't considered?" This shifts from inward (they're in my way) to outward (they're a person with their own reality).
- **Self-Betrayal Awareness:** "Is there something you know you should do in this situation that you're not doing? Sometimes our strongest frustrations come from the gap between what we sense is right and what we're actually choosing."
- **The Warner Principle:** "Heart-based change means actually BEING different, not just acting different. What would it look like to genuinely feel differently about this, not just behave differently?"

### 1.19 Conflict Resolution & Dangerous Love

**Source principles:** Conflict is our inability to collaboratively solve problems, not an inherent evil (Ford). Fear is the primary barrier to conflict transformation (Ford). Dangerous love — a love that overcomes fear in the face of conflict, seeing the humanity of others so clearly that their needs matter as much as our own (Ford). Us-preservation vs. self-preservation (Ford). Turn toward conflict rather than away from it (Ford). Gottman's Four Horsemen — criticism, contempt, defensiveness, stonewalling (Gottman via Schwartz). Self-led repair — acknowledge, empathize, commit to change (Schwartz).

**Where it lives in MyAIM:**

| Feature | PRD | Role | Status |
|---------|-----|------|--------|
| Cyrano Me | PRD-05 guided mode | Drafting communication in conflict situations | Designed |
| Higgins Navigate | PRD-05 guided mode | Navigating difficult conversations | Designed |
| Couple Meeting | PRD-16 | Structured space for relationship work | Designed |
| Family Meeting | PRD-16 | Structured space for family conflict resolution | Designed |
| ThoughtSift | PRD-05 guided mode | Processing feelings about conflict before acting | Designed |

**LiLa coaching principles (NEW):**
- **Fear Inventory:** When someone is avoiding a conversation, LiLa can help name the specific fears: "What exactly are you afraid will happen if you bring this up? Fear of the conversation itself? Fear of their reaction? Fear of what it means about the relationship?" Naming fears reduces their power.
- **Us-Preservation Reframe:** "What will happen to your relationship if you DON'T address this? Sometimes avoiding conflict is the riskiest choice."
- **Turning Toward:** LiLa coaches approach, not avoidance. "I know this is scary. But the fact that it matters to you enough to be scary means it's worth addressing."
- **Self-Led Repair (post-conflict):** LiLa can walk through the three-step repair: (1) "What did they experience from their perspective?" (2) "What sincere empathy can you express?" (3) "What specific commitment can you make about working on the parts that contributed?"
- **Four Horsemen Awareness:** LiLa can gently flag when someone's description of a conflict includes criticism, contempt, defensiveness, or stonewalling patterns — not to diagnose, but to bring awareness: "I notice you mentioned [pattern]. That's a common protective response. What do you think you were really feeling underneath that?"

### 1.20 Special Needs & Adaptive Support

**Source principles:** All behavior is communication — challenging behavior serves a communicative function, not willful defiance (Stein). Examine the environment first before attributing behavior to the child's character (Stein). Behavior escalates when individuals lack adequate communication tools (Stein). Adults who view behavior as intentional misbehavior will respond with punishment, which fails to address the underlying need (Stein).

**Where it lives in MyAIM:**

| Feature | PRD | Role | Status |
|---------|-----|------|--------|
| Caregiver Tools | Future PRD | Special Adult shift management, trackable events | Future |
| IEP/Special Needs tracking | PRD-11 (Victory Recorder) | Progress tracking for IEP goals | Designed (data field) |
| Widgets/Trackers | PRD-10 | Custom tracking for behavioral patterns, therapy goals | Designed |
| InnerWorkings — child profiles | PRD-07 | Understanding each child's processing patterns | Designed |
| Adaptive Behavior support | LiLa coaching | Environmental assessment coaching | NEW |

**LiLa coaching principles (NEW):**
- **Behavior-as-Communication Reframe:** When mom reports challenging behavior from a special needs child, LiLa's first response should be: "What might they be trying to tell you through this behavior? What need isn't being met?" Not dismissive of the difficulty — but reframing from "defiance" to "communication."
- **Environment-First Assessment:** "Before we look at what the child is doing, let's look at what's happening around them. Has anything changed in the routine, environment, or expectations?"
- **Progress celebration calibrated to the individual:** Victory Recorder's "small steps rule" is especially critical here. A child going potty independently, making eye contact during a conversation, or tolerating a transition without meltdown are enormous victories that deserve full celebration without measuring against neurotypical benchmarks.

## SECTION 4: Rhythm Design Questions & Decisions

### Morning Rhythm Open Questions

1. **Sections:** What sections ship in the default morning template? *(Recommendation provided in Section 1.2)*
2. **Task view integration:** Should the morning rhythm pull the user's preferred prioritization view, or always show a simplified "top 3"? *(Recommendation: pull their preferred view — honors their chosen framework)*
3. **Calendar integration:** Does the morning rhythm show calendar items for the day? *(Recommendation: yes, as a section. Stub until Calendar PRD.)*
4. **Morning reflection option:** Should there be a morning reflection prompt, or is morning purely forward-looking? *(Recommendation: morning is forward-looking. Reflection belongs in the evening. But user can customize — buffet principle.)*
5. **Kid-specific defaults:** What default content for kids? *(Recommendation: Play mode = encouraging message + routine checklist. Guided mode = today's tasks + encouragement. Independent = same as adult but age-appropriate.)*

### Evening Rhythm Open Questions

1. **Sections:** What sections ship in the default? *(Recommendation provided in Section 1.3)*
2. **Reflection interface:** Individual text fields per question, or one freeform block? *(Recommendation: individual fields per question with the option to expand into freeform. Each field can route independently.)*
3. **Incomplete tasks:** Should the evening rhythm show uncompleted tasks for triage? *(Recommendation: NO. Victory Recorder philosophy. Triage belongs in morning or separate task review.)*
4. **Tomorrow prep:** Pre-populated from calendar + carried tasks, or freeform intention-setting? *(Recommendation: both — show what's known, offer freeform space for intentions.)*
5. **"Answer at least 3" guideline:** Visible counter, soft suggestion, or enforced for kids? *(Open — needs discussion. Could be a soft progress indicator: "You've reflected on 2 of 3 today" without blocking dismissal.)*
6. **Dynamic prompts from LiLa:** Visually distinct or seamlessly blended? *(Recommendation: seamlessly blended. The magic is that it feels personal without being obviously AI-generated.)*
7. **Shareable reflections:** Can answers be shared with spouse or parent? *(Recommendation: opt-in per answer. A teen might choose to share a gratitude reflection with mom. A spouse might share with partner.)*

### Cross-Cutting Rhythm Decisions

1. **Per-member templates:** Do each role type (mom, dad, teen, guided, play) get different default templates, or one template that renders differently? *(Recommendation: different defaults per role type, all customizable.)*
2. **Custom rhythms:** Users can create any rhythm at any cadence (daily at custom time, specific weekday, monthly, custom interval). Content is user-defined. Mom can assign to family members. *(Confirmed in PRD-18 conversation.)*
3. **Rhythm-to-Meeting linking:** Should periodic rhythm cards (Friday Overview, Monthly Review) offer "Start a [meeting type] meeting"? *(Recommendation: yes — the rhythm is the nudge, the meeting is the deep dive.)*
4. **Section categories:** Mom picks from a library of section types when designing rhythms for herself or kids. Section types include: Guiding Star rotation, Best Intentions focus, Task preview, Calendar preview, Victory summary, Reflection prompts, Brain dump capture, Custom text/prompt, LifeLantern check-in, Scripture/quote rotation, Encouraging message, and user-created custom sections.
